FAQ

Can we talk about diversity?

Uhm, yes please!!! FemmeHacks values diversity of age, race, economic status, gender expression, size, physical ability, developmental ability, learning styles, nationality, religion, thought, citizenship status, and sexual orientation. We welcome all disciplines and interests, experience levels, and skill sets. We promote respect and do not tolerate racism, sexism, homophobia, or other discriminatory behavior or expression.
FemmeHacks acknowledges the complicated nature of the word “women,” knowing it may not work for many, and can feel unwelcoming or alienating for some. We use * to specifically and intentionally welcome trans and cis women, as well as nonbinary and gender non-conforming folks :). Equality for all, y'all.

Who can come?

Any college or high student student in the Philly area that identifies as a woman or non-binary is welcome. No experience is required!

When is it?

The actual hackathon will take place on Saturday, February 20th, but there will be team matching and hackathon-startup sessions on Friday.

What should I bring?

A computer, charger, and enthusiasm :D

Is it okay if I'm not an ~experienced~ hacker?

Yes. There will be tons of mentors, workshops, and other fellow attendees to help you out!

How many people can I take in a team?

Max of 4 people, please.

How much does it cost?

Nothing! Food, drinks, and $w@g provided. Note that there is no travel reimbursement or housing at this time. We will try to hook you up with other FemmeHackers to house you (email ameliago@seas.upenn.edu about our Hostess with the Mostest FB group) but can't guarantee anything.

I don't have a team. Can I still come?

Absolutely! You will get the chance to make one on Friday night.
